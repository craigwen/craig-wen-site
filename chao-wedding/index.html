Boston and Cambridge are inundated with great restaurants. Here are a few suggestions that are personal to us:

  • Henrietta's Table

    Charles Hotel, Harvard Square

    C: I love the lamb and cranberry walnut bread.

    P: They use seasonal and local food sources!

  • Le Petit Robert Bistro

    Columbus Ave, Boston

    C: THE BREAD. THE HOMEMADE PATE TRIO.

    P: Ditto.

  • Rod Dee

    Porter Square, Cambridge

    C: Mmm, there's nothing like Indonesian Fried Rice. They have great Tom Yum Noodles too.

    P: Great staples like Pad Thai and Pineapple Fried Rice.

  • Anna's Taqueria

    Multiple locations

    P: The extra couple minute wait for the quesadilla is well worth it.

  • Ernesto's Pizza

    North End, Boston

    C: Get a freshly made pie to share.

  • Berryline

    Multiple locations

    P: Second only to Yogurtland.

  • The Helmand

    Lechmere, Cambridge

    C: Supposedly authentic food from Afghanistan. I can't seem to deviate from the vegetarian special.

  • Mu Que Ca

    Inman Square, Cambridge

    C: Definitely try the restaurant's namesake.

  • Oishii

    South End, Boston & Chestnut Hill

    P: Best sushi in Boston! Try the South End location for fine dining and the Chestnut location for a shoulder-to-shoulder sushi experience.

  • Greek Corner

    Cambridge

    P: Great gyros, falafel, and Greek fries.

  • Masa

    South End, Boston; for Saturday brunch

    C: The BBQ mac and cheese is the best around.

    P: The Texas French Toast is a mile high and they have tasty drinks.

  • Cafe Mami

    Porter Square, Cambridge

    C: If you like juicy fried chicken, try the Tetsuta.

    P: Hamburg!

  • Hei La Moon

    Chinatown, Boston

    C: Our go-to dim sum place.

  • MuLan

    Inman Square, Cambridge

    P: Great Taiwanese restaurant with excellent dumplings, fresh or frozen!

  • Five Guys

    Natick

    C: We found that not all Five Guys restaurants are the same, and we are consistently satisfied with this one.
